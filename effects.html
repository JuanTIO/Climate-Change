Natural Disasters

Global warminig and climate change in general have caused an increase in the severity of natural disaters such as Hurricanes. The overall increase in global tempertures cause a steading in global temperature which result in a decrease of storm frequency but end up creating less frquent super storms that cause increasingly more damage as the years go on. For example hurricane Irma in comparison to any hurrican from decades prior is almost double the size.

The Artic

Since the Artic has existed, it has expireinced a anual loss and gain. This has always been true however that the net loss and gain remained relatively consistent and niether gained or lost too much. However in recent years the amount gained has decreased and the amount it has lost per year has steadily increaced. In 2012 the Artic lost about 81 tonnes but recently in 2014 it has increased to 241.1 tonnes of ice. This increase of melting ice has attributed to the rising sea leves affecting major cities such a New York, New Orleans,Guangzhou, and Mumbai.Experts predict by 2100 rising sea levels will amount to 1.5 meters; drowning these cities.

The Forests

Rising temperatures also contribute to the driving of forests closer to the poles. For example, in the Northern Hemisphere Temerate forests are being driven northward due to the increase in Temperature leading them to worse soil where specific trees and plant life suffer greatly. The changing percipatation is also attributing to the changing rainfalls that threaten certain ecosysems like rainforests. This alongside deforestation due to wood harvesting and other things, is leading to an ever shrinking zone of habilabilty for our forests.
